About Me

I am Geetika, a PhD scholar at the Distributed Computing Learning Lab (DCLL) at Indraprastha Institute of Information Technology (IIIT-D). My research focuses on Mathematical Optimization in Machine Learning, with a particular interest in Distributed and Federated Machine Learning.

Research Interests

I am deeply engrossed in the convergence analysis of algorithms deployed in Federated and Distributed Learning. My passion lies in exploring and analyzing the theoretical aspects of these algorithms to enhance their robustness.

Projects

  • Project 1: Towards Parameter-Free Federated Learning - Developed two line-search based optimization algorithms for Federated Learning and performed convergence analysis.
  • Project 2: Convergence Analysis of Fixed-Point Iterative Processes under Operators with Property (E) - Proved some weak and strong convergence results for the iterative process with respect to the class of Garcia-Falset generalized non-expansive operators.
  • Project 3: Detecting PII Data In Educational Dataset - Employed DeBERTa_v3_small_en model to use its Disentangled Attention Mechanism and its contextual understanding to detect PII data in the educational dataset.
